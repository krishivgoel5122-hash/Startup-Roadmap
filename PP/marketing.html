Market Research

Market research helps entrepreneurs understand whether enough people are willing and able to buy their product in a competitive environment.

Why Market Research Matters

A strong idea in a weak market will still fail. Market research prevents entrepreneurs from building products that too few people want or are willing to pay for.

Economically, this stage reduces uncertainty and prevents inefficient use of scarce resources such as capital and time.

Understanding the Economics

Demand refers to how many consumers want a product and can afford it at a given price. If demand is low, even a well-built product may struggle to survive.

Supply represents existing alternatives in the market. High supply increases competition, often lowering prices and profit margins.

Market size determines revenue potential. A niche market can work, but only if pricing and costs align with limited demand.

Real-Life Application

Imagine a student planning to start an online tutoring service. At first glance, demand may seem high because many students need help. However, market research may reveal that the space is already saturated with low-cost or free alternatives.

From an economic perspective, high supply combined with moderate demand puts downward pressure on prices. This limits profit potential unless the entrepreneur offers clear differentiation.

By researching competitors, pricing levels, and customer willingness to pay, the entrepreneur avoids entering a market where revenue would not justify the time and effort invested.

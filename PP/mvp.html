Minimum Viable Product (MVP)

MVPs allow startups to test assumptions while minimizing financial risk.

Economic Concept: Costâ€“Benefit Analysis

Why MVPs Matter

Building a full product without validation is expensive and risky. MVPs allow learning when mistakes are cheapest.

Understanding the Economics

MVPs apply costâ€“benefit analysis by comparing the cost of building features with the learning gained.

A simpler MVP often delivers higher economic value than a complex product built too early.

Real-Life Application

A student planning a fitness app might initially imagine a complex platform with tracking, social features, and AI recommendations. Building this fully would require significant time and money.

Instead, an MVP could be a simple landing page or basic app that tracks one key metric. This allows the entrepreneur to test whether users actually engage with the concept.

Economically, this reduces risk by limiting upfront costs while still generating valuable information about user behaviour.
